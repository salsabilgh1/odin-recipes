Couscous

Description

Couscous is a traditional North African dish made from steamed semolina wheat granules. It's light and fluffy when cooked and is often served as a base for vegetables, meats, or stews. To prepare couscous, you usually steam it or soak it in hot water or broth, allowing it to absorb the liquid and soften. It’s commonly paired with a hearty vegetable or lamb stew spiced with cumin, coriander, and cinnamon for a rich and aromatic meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup couscous
  • 1 cup water or vegetable broth
  • 1 tbsp olive oil or butter
  • Salt to taste
  • Mixed vegetables (e.g. carrots, zucchini, chickpeas, onions)
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric

Steps

  1. Boil water or broth with olive oil and salt.
  2. Pour the boiling liquid over couscous in a bowl, cover, and let sit for 5–10 minutes.
  3. Fluff couscous with a fork.
  4. Meanwhile, sauté chopped vegetables with spices until tender.
  5. Mix vegetables into the couscous and serve warm.
